Arthritis & joint pain in senior dogs

If your old dog is slowing down, stiff in the mornings, or hesitating at the stairs, you're in the right place. Here's what I've learned about keeping achy joints comfortable — gently, and without panic.

Arthritis is one of the most common parts of a dog growing old — and one of the most manageable. Small changes at home often bring real comfort. This page gathers my gentlest, most useful guidance in one place.

Where to start

Joint pain creeps in slowly, so it’s easy to mistake for “just getting old.” But stiffness isn’t something your dog has to simply live with. Between a good conversation with your vet, a few thoughtful changes at home, and the right bed and traction, most senior dogs can move more easily and rest more deeply.

The goal isn’t to turn back the clock. It’s to make today comfortable — and tomorrow a little easier.

Signs to watch for

You know your dog better than anyone. These are the quiet changes owners most often notice:

  • Stiffness or a slow “warm-up” after lying down, especially in the morning
  • Hesitating at stairs, jumps, or slick floors they used to take in stride
  • Shorter walks, lagging behind, or sitting down partway
  • Licking a joint, or a subtle shift in how they hold a leg
  • More naps, less play, a little grumpiness when touched

None of these are a diagnosis — they’re a reason to check in with your vet, who can confirm what’s going on and rule out anything else.
